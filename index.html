San Francisco Crime Atlas

Exploring crime patterns in San Francisco - One of the most iconic cities in the United States of America

This page presents a small visual story built from historical San Francisco police incident reports. You can scan long-term trends, explore neighborhood-level geography, and compare how incident intensity changes across the day.

Urban shape San Francisco is often described as a “seven-by-seven-mile” city.
Terrain More than 50 hills create strong neighborhood contrasts.
Climate Fog and coastal winds produce sharp microclimate differences.

Focus crime categories

This analysis examines nine incident types chosen for their frequency and significance in understanding urban crime patterns. These span property crime, violent crime, drug-related incidents, and administrative cases:

  • Larceny/theft — The largest category; includes shoplifting, car break-ins, and personal theft
  • Non-criminal — Administrative reports, lost property, and other non-crime incidents
  • Assault — Violent interpersonal crimes, including simple and aggravated assault
  • Vehicle theft — Stolen cars and motorcycles
  • Drug/narcotic — Drug possession, manufacturing, and sales offenses
  • Vandalism — Damage to property and public infrastructure
  • Warrants — Outstanding arrest warrants and related incidents
  • Burglary — Breaking and entering of residences and commercial spaces
  • Suspicious occurrence — Suspicious activity requiring investigation

Visualizations

Explore the landscape of crime in San Francisco through three visualizations: a static long-term trend chart, an interactive dot map, and an hourly profile chart.

Long-term trend snapshot

High-level yearly movement in focus crime categories. Useful for spotting trends, major events, and seasonal changes.

Interactive dot map

Spatial distribution of incidents across the city. The plot is focused on the first month of 2025 to declutter the output.

Interactive hourly profile

Normalized incident levels by hour of day. Great for comparing day/night behavioral patterns.

Method & context

The visualizations are based on historical SFPD incident-report data. The goal is descriptive insight: where incidents cluster, when activity peaks, and how broad trends evolve through time.
